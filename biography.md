Shalom Keshet (pseudonym: Natty) is an author and independent researcher whose interests span mathematics, computation, scientific writing, and theoretical aspects of space and physics.

His early work includes literary publications and participation in educational and research-oriented programs. His projects and creative work have been recognized through selections and awards associated with organizations such as Google, NASA-affiliated outreach initiatives, and IIT Madras.

He is the author of several books, including *The Lost Boy*, later translated into Slovenian, and *Herman Potočnik’s Space Odyssey*, a science-focused work examining early astronautical theory and the contributions of Herman Potočnik Noordung.

Alongside writing, he engages in independent research in mathematics and computation, with a focus on symbolic methods, functional transformations, and theoretical modeling. His academic website is intentionally structured to emphasize research work, with biographical context provided separately when requested.
